# Jurisdiction

"{" <ISO 3166-1> [[ "." | ":"] <state or province> [ "." | ":" <county | district> ]]] [["ยง" <legislation & year [- <pinhole>]]  "}"

The distinction is that "." lower level is subordinate to higher whereas ":" is strongly precedent but not binding, mostly seen in commonwealth. There's a carveout provision "^" which are exemptions such as Waitangi Treaty provisions or native American reservations which are legally distinct. Where there is no named district a geometric shape may be placeholder.

# Legal Research 

1. Doctrinal Legal Research&{Design/Develop/Deploy} - what is the law on a given issue
2. Empirical Legal R&D^3 - how effective is this law
3. Analytical Legal R&D^3 - why is the law in that way (critical analysis)
4. Descriptive/Historical Legal Research - what law exists/evolves over time/space/field
5. Comparative Legal Research - where law differs/changes
6. Applied Legal Research - who/what does the law put at risk
7. Quant/Qual Legal Research - how/basis of determining the law
8. Computational Legal Research - exploration of novel forms of law/rules (via synthesis / simulation / stimulation)
